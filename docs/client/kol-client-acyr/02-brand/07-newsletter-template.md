# Newsletter template

The shape of every issue. Built on example #7 in [[02-brand/05-writing-examples|writing-examples]] — *From the studio — February*. The voice is the studio's own; the structure is repeatable.

Use as a starter when drafting a new issue. Skip sections that have nothing to say this month. Don't pad. A short issue is better than a long one.

---

## How to use

1. Duplicate the **Template body** section below into a new file or directly into MailerLite.
2. Replace each `{{ placeholder }}` with the month's content.
3. Strike any block that doesn't apply this month — better than a section with hollow filler.
4. Read once against [[02-brand/04-writing-guidelines|writing-guidelines]] before sending.
5. Save as a MailerLite draft campaign; reuse the layout for every issue.

---

## Editorial guardrails

- **Length.** Target 180–280 words in the body. A January issue can be shorter than a March-before-show issue, but anything past 350 words probably has two issues in it.
- **Cadence.** Monthly. Subject line: `From the studio — {{ Month }}`. Sentence case, no exclamation, no emoji.
- **Voice anchor.** Pull from [[02-brand/04-writing-guidelines|writing-guidelines]]. Quiet, considered, anti-trend. Specifics over claims. One em-dash per paragraph max.
- **"We"** for the studio. Not "I". The studio writes; Ýr signs the reply chain.
- **No urgency, no countdowns, no "shop now".** If a piece is shipping or showing, name the date and the place. The reader handles the rest.
- **Reply-friendly close.** Every issue invites a reply. "Reply to this email if you want to talk to a person — it goes to Ýr."

## Section notes

### Subject line

Always `From the studio — {{ Month }}`. The reader's inbox should recognise the pattern. Don't get clever with subject lines per issue.

### Opening paragraph

Two beats: **state of the studio** (what's on the cutting table, the rack, the floor) and **material logistics** (what's arrived, what's en route, what's delayed). Concrete nouns; named places. If nothing is delayed, don't manufacture drama. If a delay matters, say so without apology.

### "This month" bullets

Em-dash bullets, not hyphen, not asterisk. One line each, two at the absolute max. Each bullet is a date + a place + a fact. The reader infers the importance.

Skip the section if there's truly nothing — but most months have something: a shoot, a fitting, a piece of press, a guest in the studio.

### Order status paragraph

Optional. Use when there are open made-to-measure or pre-order pieces. Skip if everything that month is regular stock shipping on schedule. When in doubt, write the line and cut it on read-through.

### Close

The sign-off is fixed. Don't redraft it. The fixed close is part of the rhythm — the reader knows where the issue ends, and the invitation to reply works because it's there every time.

`— The studio` on its own line, then the email. No phone number unless the issue specifically asks for press contact.

---

## What this template is not

- **Not a campaign template.** Sales, promotions, discount codes, "limited time" framing — none of that. The voice doesn't sell. The garment carries the rest.
- **Not a digest.** No "what we've been reading", no listicles, no "x things you should know about". The studio writes about its own work, not about the world.
- **Not heavy with imagery.** One image at most per issue, used when it adds a fact (the new fabric arrived; the lookbook shoot). Decorative imagery dilutes the voice.

---

## Where to keep the MailerLite version

Build the campaign once in MailerLite (using this doc as the source). Save it as a draft / template. Every subsequent issue duplicates the template, swaps the placeholders, sends. If voice or structure changes, update this doc *and* the MailerLite template together — don't let them drift.

# Styleguide text — rewrite

Working rewrite of `styleguide-text.md`, drafted as a real-prose exercise to surface the brand voice. The original carries marketing residue from older copy; this draft tries to hold the descriptors the studio actually wants to sound like — Paris/New York editorial cool, calm and knowledgeable, sculpting its own reality, unpretentious, kind, educative, generous.

Not final. Iterate from here.

## 02 — bio

### Designer and director

Two practices, one person.

Born in Oslo, 1984. Moved to Reykjavík at five and stayed. Now works between fashion and film, with neither apologising for the other.

She studied fashion at the Iceland Academy of the Arts and graduated in 2011. The graduation collection caught the eye of the press, started YR Collections, and led to Another Creation, co‑founded in 2013. Four shows at Reykjavík Fashion Festival followed; a stint at Designer's Nest during Copenhagen Fashion Week in 2011; selected from over 200 applicants for StartupReykjavík; and a Creative Business Cup award the same year — for combining the creative side with the commercial.

In 2024 she finished a BA at the Iceland Film School, focused on Creative Technology. Her short, *Agnes Iwaz*, won Best Movie in the same category. The films move through costume, direction, edit, sound, and grade — built with a designer's instinct for material in motion.

The thread between the two: an interest in how things are made, and a refusal to make more than is needed.

> "The essence of cinema is editing — the combination of what can be extraordinary images of people during emotional moments, or images in a general sense, put together in a kind of alchemy."

— Walter Murch, often quoted in the studio.

## 01 — about

### About Another Creation

A womenswear label from Reykjavík. Made by hand, in small numbers, since 2013.

The work stands against overproduction. Garments are cut and stitched in the studio in small runs that don't get re‑cut next season. Sourcing, finishing, and packaging all hold to that — less of everything, made better.

Alongside the collections, the studio designs for film, theatre, concerts, and the Iceland Dance Company, and makes dresses and coats to measure for women who'd rather wear something the world hasn't seen.

#### Made by hand

Each garment is constructed by hand. Production scales by intention, not by volume. The answer to making too much is to make less.

#### For long ownership

Pieces are designed for years, not seasons. The label resists the trend cycle and the disposable rhythm around it, in favour of garments that earn their place in a wardrobe over time.

#### Reykjavík studio

The work is made in Reykjavík. The city is the studio — the location, the rhythm, the light. Not a marketing claim.

## 02 — voice

### Voice

Quiet, considered, anti‑trend.

The voice favours specifics over claims, materials over marketing, the considered phrase over the loud one. It addresses the woman who reads the label, not the one who chases the season. It describes; it doesn't sell. The garment carries the rest.

---

## 03 — look

### Look

Editorial. Restrained. Photo‑driven.

Photography leans toward landscape, material, and figure — natural light over studio gloss. Compositions are direct, not styled. Type is set quietly, with deliberate spacing. The palette is small and confident: burgundy and cream, anchored. The brand never ornaments where it can let the garment speak.

## 06 — color

### Palette

Greyscale carries the structure. Burgundy and cream carry the identity.

The system splits colour into two roles. Greyscale handles the backbone — surfaces, ink, dividers, canvas. The brand palette is narrow: burgundy as the identity hue, cream as a warm neutral.

#### Greyscale

The canvas and the structural ink. A 10‑stop ramp, kept until the opacity‑hex primitive is reintroduced.

#### Burgundy

The identity hue. Anchored at 200. Named: 200 Burgundy · 300 Dark Maroon · 400 Deep Wine.

#### Cream

The neutral. Anchored on Champagne (300) and Sand Gold (400). For warm‑light editorial moments and tinting.

#### Use

Greyscale carries the canvas and the structural ink. Burgundy names the identity. Cream sits as a neutral surface for warm‑leaning compositions. Apply brand colour with restraint — never decoratively.

## 07 — typography

### Type

Right Grotesk across the system. One family, four cuts.

Right Grotesk is the brand voice. Narrow carries display, title, and H1. Compact carries H2–H4 and the lede. Mono is for labels and code. The base cut carries the body. The family was designed for editorial set — quiet by default.

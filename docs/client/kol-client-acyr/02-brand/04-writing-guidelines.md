# Writing guidelines

How Another Creation sounds. The rules half of the pair — companion to `writing-examples.md`, which shows the voice in use across ten situations.

Read both together: this doc names what the prose is doing; the examples doc shows it doing it.

---

## Voice

Quiet, considered, anti-trend. Editorial cool — closer to Paris and New York fashion journalism than to product marketing. Calm, knowledgeable, and a little wry. Generous with information; unpretentious with itself. The voice doesn't sell. It describes. The garment carries the rest.

The reader is treated as a peer. Specifics are offered without preamble. The brand assumes intelligence and rewards it.

---

## What makes the prose work

Observations from writing the styleguide rewrite and the worked examples — things to notice, not rules to enforce.

1. **Restraint with adjectives.** When facts carry, modifiers get in the way. `Heavy boiled wool, lined in cotton.` reads better than `Beautifully soft, premium-quality wool with a luxurious cotton lining.`

2. **Specifics over claims.** Year, place, institution, award, mill, fabric weight. Specifics tell the reader you know the thing. Claims tell the reader you want them to believe you do.

3. **Sentence length variation.** Mostly short and declarative; one longer thought per paragraph; rarely two long ones in a row. The rhythm is calm because the cadence holds. Don't end every sentence the same way.

4. **Em-dash as sharpener, not tic.** One em-dash per paragraph at most. The dash works because it earns the pause; three in five lines and it loses the work it's doing. Spaced both sides: ` — `.

5. **Quiet self-awareness over manifesto.** A small dry note ("Not a marketing claim", "with neither apologising for the other") does more for tone than three sentences about what the brand believes. The reader notices the brand is in on the joke without the brand having to point.

6. **Trust the reader.** Phrases like "women who'd rather wear something the world hasn't seen" assume the reader can finish the thought. Over-explaining condescends.

7. **Geography is fact, not identity.** Reykjavík is where the studio is. Iceland is where the country is. Neither needs to be a brand statement. Drop "Icelandic lineage", "Nordic heritage", and anything similar.

8. **Don't repeat the descriptor inside the piece.** The voice is "quiet, considered, anti-trend" — use it once, in the right place; don't re-declare it every paragraph.

9. **Reference culture by passing mention, not by branding.** Quoting Walter Murch is fine; explaining who Walter Murch is, in a piece about cutting linen, is not. The reader looks it up or doesn't — both are fine.

10. **No exclamation, no urgency, no claimed status.** No "right now", "today only", "industry-leading", "iconic". The voice never raises itself. If something is iconic, the reader will think so without being told.

## House style

- **Em-dashes** — spaced, ` — `, sparing.
- **Sentence case** for headings, buttons, navigation. Not Title Case. Not UPPERCASE.
- **Brand name**: `Another Creation`. **Designer**: `Ýr Þrastardóttir` (diacritics always).
- **No exclamation points.**
- **Oxford comma** — yes. `linen, cotton, and wool`.
- **"and"** over **"&"**. Ampersand only inside lockup labels by exception.
- **Curly quotes** in prose. Straight quotes in code and form inputs only.
- **Periods** on full sentences only. Nav labels, button text, and form labels take none.

## Words

| Favour            | Avoid                  | Why                                                       |
|-------------------|------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| considered        | curated                | Describes work vs. decorative                             |
| handmade          | luxury                 | Method vs. marketing word                                 |
| small numbers     | exclusive              | Fact vs. gate                                             |
| made for years    | iconic                 | Claimed durability vs. claimed status                     |
| material          | premium                | Name the thing; don't grade it                            |
| by hand           | drops / capsule        | Method vs. streetwear cadence                             |
| deliberate        | effortless             | Work is visible; pretending it isn't is dishonest         |
| Reykjavík         | "Nordic luxury"        | Geography as fact, not identity claim                     |
| in small numbers  | shop now               | Production framing vs. clickbait                          |
| by intention      | limited time           | Maker's pacing vs. fast-fashion grammar                   |

Also avoid: amazing, incredible, stunning, ultimate, perfect, best, transformative, elevated, sophisticated, timeless (overused), authentic (overused), unique, must-have.
